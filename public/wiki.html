Overview

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System, closest to the Sun. Its heavily cratered surface resembles the Moon and retains little atmosphere. Surface temperatures swing from 427 C at day to -173 C at night, the largest variation of any planet.

Active missions

Mariner 10 (NASA)
Flybys 1974-1975
First spacecraft to visit Mercury. Three flybys between 1974 and 1975. Mapped ~45% of the surface.
MESSENGER (NASA)
Orbit 2011-2015
First orbiter. Mapped 100% of Mercury, characterized composition, and confirmed water ice in permanently shadowed polar craters.
BepiColombo (ESA/JAXA)
En route, orbital insertion 2026
Joint ESA/JAXA mission with two orbiters: MPO (ESA) and MMO/Mio (JAXA). Will study surface, interior, and magnetosphere after insertion.

Notable features

Caloris Basin
One of the largest impact basins in the Solar System, 1,550 km across. Antipodal "weird terrain" resulted from seismic focusing.
Polar ice deposits
Confirmed water ice in permanently shadowed craters near both poles. Detected via radar and MESSENGER neutron spectrometer.
Hollows
Unique irregular depressions unlike anything on Moon. May result from sublimation of volatile material.
